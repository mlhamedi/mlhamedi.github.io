Your Forever Home

By Koi

For as long as I can remember, I've wanted a pet. Both my grandparents and my aunt lived in the country and had stray cats around, and every summer I would spend a great deal of time coaxing, feeding, and playing with these cats. I could only imagine what it would be like to have a cat of my own, in my own home. My parents weren't so thrilled about the idea of a cat, and my brother was an avid dog person. 

At the age of seven, we got lucky and were given a two year old Golden Retriever named Lucky. In my heart, I still wanted a cat of my own and the idea of having a large dog instead was scary. But Lucky was the sweetest dog, and though mischevious to no end, had the kindest heart of any animal I'd known. Lucky and I became very close friends.

My senior year of high school, Lucky got sick. He was close to fifteen years old by then, and it is very common for Golden Retrievers to get tumors. Unbeknownst to us, Lucky had five different tumors. Losing him was one of the most difficult loses I've experienced. While a pet and not a person, Lucky was still family.

As I was soon to leave for college, I never got another pet. There could be no animal to replace Lucky, but my love for animals didn't die with him. I've come to love dogs, particulalry Golden Retrievers, but in my heart there has always been a soft spot for cats. Just before my 23rd birthday, I went to a local shelter and found a sweet, loving little creature. 

We named him Gustaf. Sometimes we call him Asparagus. He's seven months old and still learning what "don't bite my toes" means. He is a very sweet and adorable kitten. I was very scared to bring him into my home since I'd never had a cat of my own before, but he is sweet and kind and so deserving of a home. 

Gustaf
